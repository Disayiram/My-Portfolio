Twitter Data Analysis

Analysed New Year's resolution tweets using natural language processing (NLP), statistical methods, and machine learning. The project revealed key trends in user behavior, sentiment patterns, and regional activity, offering actionable insights for businesses and researchers to enhance their social media strategies.

  • Tech Stack & Techniques: Python, Pandas, scikit-learn, VADER, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Data Preprocessing, Statistical Analysis, Sentiment Analysis
  • Highlights:
    • **Popular Categories**: Identified the most common New Year's resolution categories (personal growth, fitness, time management). These insights can guide businesses in tailoring marketing campaigns and product offerings during the New Year season.
    • **Sentiment Trends**: Conducted sentiment analysis with 91% accuracy (AUC score), providing an understanding of user emotions. This helps brands design messaging that resonates with positive and aspirational sentiments.
    • **Regional Heatwaves**: Visualized geographic tweet activity, identifying highly engaged regions. Businesses can target these locations with region-specific promotions and advertisements.
  • Impact: The project provided actionable insights into user motivations and trends, offering valuable data for businesses and researchers targeting social media campaigns during seasonal periods.

Weather Trends Analysis in Greater London

Conducted an in-depth statistical analysis of long-term weather trends in Greater London from 1960 to 2019. By leveraging historical climate data and advanced analytical techniques, the study revealed significant temperature trends, seasonal cloud cover patterns, and correlations between key climate variables.

  • Tech Stack & Techniques: R, Python Time Series Analysis, Regression Analysis, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  • Key Insights:
    • Significant temperature increases (mean, maximum, and minimum), highlighting long-term warming trends.
    • Seasonal cloud cover patterns showed higher levels in winter and lower levels in summer, offering insights for energy and agricultural planning.
    • Positive correlation between humidity and precipitation; negative correlation between cloud cover and solar radiation, providing data for climate resilience strategies.
  • Impact: Findings support environmental planning, renewable energy strategies, and climate adaptation policies for sustainable development.

Addressing Bias in Large Language Models

Large Language Models (LLMs) are powerful tools driving modern AI advancements, but they often reflect and amplify societal biases, posing ethical challenges. This project pioneers a systematic approach to identifying and reducing gender bias in text generation for occupational roles, paving the way for fairer and more inclusive AI systems.

  • Tech Stack & Techniques: Python, Large Language Models (LLMs), Prompt Engineering, Bias Identification, Bias Analysis, Quantitative Analysis, Bias Reduction
  • Key Insights:
    • Bias Reduction Success: Gender bias in generated text was reduced for most occupations, with improvements ranging from 2.67% (sheriff) to 75.33% (teacher).
    • Most Biased Roles: Initial male bias was highest in cook, mechanic, and construction worker, while teacher had the highest female bias.
    • Significant Improvements: Teacher and cook roles showed significant reductions in bias, with 75.33% and 54.67% improvements, respectively.
  • Impact: This project contributes to the field of ethical AI by demonstrating effective strategies for mitigating societal biases in LLM-generated text, setting the stage for further advancements in responsible AI research.
